# Stock Analysis Guide

## Valuation Metrics

| Metric | What it means | Good range (general) |
|--------|--------------|---------------------|
| Trailing P/E | Price / last 12mo earnings | 15-25 (varies by sector) |
| Forward P/E | Price / projected earnings | Lower than trailing = growth expected |
| PEG Ratio | P/E / earnings growth rate | < 1 = undervalued, > 2 = expensive |
| P/B | Price / book value | < 3 (varies; tech often higher) |
| P/S | Price / revenue | < 5 for most sectors |
| EV/EBITDA | Enterprise value / EBITDA | 10-15 typical |

## Profitability Metrics

| Metric | What it means | Strong |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Gross Margin | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue | > 40% |
| Operating Margin | Operating income / Revenue | > 20% |
| Profit Margin | Net income / Revenue | > 15% |
| ROE | Net income / Shareholder equity | > 15% |
| ROA | Net income / Total assets | > 5% |

## Growth Metrics

| Metric | Interpretation |
|--------|---------------|
| Revenue Growth | YoY revenue change; > 10% is solid |
| Earnings Growth | YoY EPS change; should outpace revenue growth |
| EPS (trailing vs forward) | Forward > trailing = analysts expect growth |

## Balance Sheet Health

| Metric | Healthy range |
|--------|--------------|
| Debt-to-Equity | < 1.0 (< 0.5 is conservative) |
| Current Ratio | > 1.5 (can meet short-term obligations) |
| Total Cash vs Total Debt | More cash = stronger position |

## Analyst Consensus

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| strong_buy | Very bullish consensus |
| buy | Bullish |
| hold | Neutral |
| sell / strong_sell | Bearish |

## Price Context

- **vs 52-week range**: Near high = momentum or overextended; near low = value or trouble
- **vs analyst targets**: Below mean target = potential upside; above = limited upside
- **Period change %**: Compare to S&P 500 (~10% annually) as benchmark

## Sector Benchmarks

Different sectors have different norms:
- **Tech**: Higher P/E (25-40), higher margins, lower dividends
- **Financials**: Lower P/E (10-15), focus on ROE and book value
- **Utilities**: Low growth, high dividends, stable margins
- **Healthcare**: Wide P/E range, focus on pipeline and revenue growth
- **Consumer**: Moderate P/E, focus on margins and brand strength
